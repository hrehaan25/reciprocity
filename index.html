How a Secure Skill Exchange Works

Student Skill Submission üìù

A participating student submits their personal skill profile and a specific skill request via the secure, school-verified portal. The platform instantly checks for existing matches in the network database.

Administrative & Moderator Approval üîí

Before any match is processed, the student's submission is flagged to the designated School Moderator (Principal/Dean/Guardian) for a mandatory 24-hour review period. The Moderator confirms the student's eligibility, guardian consent, and the appropriateness of the submitted skills.

Note: No contact information is exchanged at this stage. Safety check is paramount.

Once both submissions are approved by their respective Moderators, the proprietary algorithm runs the match. The system then generates a secure introductory email (containing only school-approved contact details and a designated meeting protocol) to both matched students and their respective Moderators/Guardians.

The students connect using a school-approved method. Reciprocity's **Secure Communication Relay** (Module 2) ensures that all digital conversation is logged, audited, and remains within the platform's safety guidelines, protecting PII (Personally Identifiable Information) at all times.

Safety & Compliance Protocol

1. System-Level Data Protection

The Reciprocity platform employs a privacy-by-design architecture to ensure student safety is non-negotiable.

  1. Data Minimization: We only collect the essential PII (Name, School ID, School Email) required for administrative vetting and match initiation.
  2. Secure Data Layer: Student skill profiles and match data are stored using end-to-end encryption.
  3. Email Proxy System: For initial contact, a system-generated, auditable email proxy is used. No peer ever sees another's raw, personal email address until all institutional checks are complete.
  4. Annual Audits: The platform undergoes mandatory annual compliance and security penetration testing by a third-party firm.

2. Moderator Responsibilities and Workflow

Moderator accounts are privileged and require annual re-verification. Moderators are the first line of defense in the safety matrix.

  1. Mandatory 24-Hour Review: Moderators are required to review and approve/reject all student skill submissions within 24 hours of entry. Unreviewed submissions are automatically withdrawn.
  2. Conflict Resolution Escalation: Any automatic flag raised by the Conflict Resolution Tool (Module 4) immediately creates a priority alert on the Moderator Action Center dashboard, requiring prompt investigation and digital journaling of the resolution steps taken.
  3. Consent Verification: Moderators must confirm that the student's Guardian Consent Portal (GCP) status is "Verified and Active" before approving any match request.
  4. Exchange Protocol Setting: The Moderator is responsible for defining the acceptable exchange parameters (e.g., "Virtual Exchange Only," "Must use school-supervised video platform," "In-person allowed only at school library").

3. Student Code of Conduct and Sanctions

All participating students are bound by a strict, zero-tolerance code of conduct focusing on respect, professionalism, and appropriate communication boundaries. Violations are managed by the student's home institution.

  1. Zero-Tolerance Policy: Any attempt to solicit or share personal, unverified contact information (mobile numbers, private social media links) is an immediate, reportable offense.
  2. Professionalism: All communication must remain focused on the skill exchange topic. Off-topic, harassing, or sexually explicit content results in immediate permanent banishment from the platform.
  3. Reporting Mandate: Students are obligated to report any suspicious or uncomfortable communication to their respective Moderator immediately. Failure to report a breach of conduct can also lead to suspension.

4. Platform Audit and Record Keeping

Reciprocity maintains permanent, non-editable logs of all key transactions for compliance purposes, accessible only to authorized institutional Moderators and Reciprocity's Compliance Officer.

  1. Audit Trail: A full digital record is kept of the date/time of skill submission, Moderator approval, match algorithm run, and all Secure Communication Relay messages.
  2. Retention Policy: All audit logs are retained for a minimum of seven years, irrespective of a school's partnership status, to comply with long-term accountability requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the "Database Match" protect student privacy? +

The core of Reciprocity's security is that student PII (Personally Identifiable Information) is never visible to their peer until the system initiates the controlled connection. The database performs the skill-to-skill match using anonymized profiles. Only upon mutual Moderator approval is a **system-generated, auditable email proxy** exchanged for initial, supervised contact. The student's real contact information is protected until the school approves the exchange parameters.

What is the role of the School Moderator (Administrator/Guardian)? +

The Moderator is the essential, trusted third party. They are responsible for vetting the student's eligibility (academic standing, behavior), approving the appropriateness of the skill being offered/requested, and setting the exchange terms (location, time, supervision) for the learning exchange. Reciprocity provides the Secure Communication Relay and the digital audit trail, but day-to-day oversight of the students falls under the partner institution's standard safety guidelines. Our role is to enable the secure connection; the institution's role is to supervise the interaction.

Is this platform compliant with Indian school data security standards and GDPR/COPPA principles? +

Yes. We adhere to stringent data protection guidelines that align with Indian regulatory requirements for student data, incorporating principles from international standards like GDPR for data sovereignty and COPPA for protecting minors' online privacy. We employ end-to-end encryption, rely on dual-layer consent (student and school/guardian), and maintain a strict data minimization policy. Our annual compliance audits ensure ongoing adherence to evolving standards.

What happens if a student is matched, but the other student's school does not approve the match? +

If a Moderator from one school rejects a match (for any reason, including scheduling or policy incompatibility), the match is immediately terminated by the system. The original requesting student is then returned to the matching queue to find a new peer. No contact information is ever exchanged in a rejected match scenario, and both Moderators receive an automatic system notification explaining the match termination.
